Context

I joined Evernote as a product designer in 2018 and grew into a senior role over nearly five years. My focus was the mobile experience — iOS and Android — at a time when Evernote was going through a major product evolution.

The product had accumulated years of feature debt. The design system was fragmented across platforms. And user expectations for mobile had fundamentally shifted.

"Our mobile experience needed to feel like a product from 2022, not 2014."

The mobile redesign

I led the redesign of the iOS and Android apps — a project that touched navigation, the note editor, search, tags, notebooks, and the home screen. The work was done in close collaboration with the mobile PM, engineering, and UX research.

[ Before / After — Evernote iOS navigation and home screen ]

We ran user testing at multiple points, iterated on prototypes, and shipped incrementally — never a big-bang release. Each feature went through weekly design reviews and eng pairing sessions.

[ Tag experience redesign — published case study on Evernote blog ]

Building the design system

As the mobile team grew, I built the mobile design system from scratch — components, tokens, documentation, and guidelines for both iOS and Android. It became the foundation every subsequent feature was built on.

[ Mobile design system — component library overview ]

What I shipped

Over nearly five years: dozens of shipped features, a full mobile redesign, a mobile design system, mentorship of junior designers, weekly stand-ups and design reviews, and direct partnership with engineering and product leadership.
