The Problem

Operators have analytics tools. They just can't trust them.

Small multi-location operators have analytics tools. They just can't trust them. Events misfire. Funnels are incomplete. Conversions get lumped. Most tools show numbers and assume the data is clean — they never tell you when it isn't. Operators make decisions on data they can't verify, and they don't know they can't verify it.

"Other tools show you your numbers. Calibrate shows you whether to trust them."
The Insight

The problem isn't the charts. It's the posture.

Dashboards put raw data in front of people and make them figure it out. I flipped it — built a feed-based product where AI personalities surface the right signal at the right time, in editorial voice. Characters like @SEO, @Conversions, and @Social each author content from their domain. Closer to a smart financial publication than a BI tool.

Screenshot: Home feed — editorial cards authored by AI characters
The Workflow Audit

The audit contradicted my own assumptions.

Midway through, I audited the app against real operator tasks — Monday morning triage, initiative progress check, funnel diagnosis across locations. The audit contradicted my own assumptions. The architecture needed a flip: Dashboard becomes the raw-data explorer; @Analytics becomes a domain character whose Summary tab surfaces the scored audit view.

That's the real case study. Not a beautiful design system — identifying what the user actually needed and rebuilding for that.

Screenshot: Dashboard — raw data layer with filters, time range, location
What I Built

Four destinations. Thirteen characters. One system.

Four top-level destinations: Home (consumption feed), Dashboard (raw data), Calibrate (approvals + decisions), Reports (shareable editorial summaries). A personality system of 13 AI characters. An initiative tracking system where each campaign gets a full profile — stats, posts, tasks, timeline. An 18-component data viz library built from scratch.

Screenshot: Profile page — initiative tracking with editorial voice
The System

A system built for voice.

Color, type, and component patterns that let every insight feel written, not rendered.
