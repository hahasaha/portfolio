The problem

90% mobile traffic hitting a desktop-first site. No lead capture. No local SEO. Every visitor the business earned was leaking out.

The constraints

No in-house dev. Webflow only. Six locations, one design system. Had to ship fast and scale.

The decisions

Mobile-first by default. One CTA above fold. Lead capture before membership. SEO as a growth channel, not an afterthought.

Case Study 02 of 02

Inkwell
/ WDI.

Enterprise UX for a major entertainment client. Joined mid-flight, diagnosed fast, shipped meaningful interaction improvements under a tight deadline.

Enterprise UX Interaction design In-flight NDA
The situation
Joined mid-flight.
No design handoff.
One designer.

A due diligence platform for a major entertainment company. Complex GIS mapping, scenario comparison, flag annotation, AI assistant — all in flight. My job: assess fast, earn trust, ship meaningful work before the deadline.

Process

Started with a
whiteboard.
Then built it.

The comparison view was the highest-leverage problem. Before designing anything, I mapped out what data actually matters when analysts compare three scenarios side by side. This FigJam became the blueprint.

Early-stage brainstorm → shipped UI
↳ Before
Three scenarios, comparables listed. No narrative, no scoring, no insights.
↳ After
Scenario Insights, Top Cost Drivers, Scores, full flag breakdown. Same data — completely different decision-making tool.
Interaction design

One modal.
Three states.
Full system.

Inherited a bare, oversized modal with no design. Added comments, pin, and hide — each with distinct color states. Orange for pinned, red for hidden. Rebuilt the width and stacked the threshold section to clean up the layout.

Click to zoom
↳ Rulebook
Built from scratch. Category filter pills, expandable rules, type tags. Gave analysts a way to understand the rules driving every flag.
↳ Action Bar
Dynamic contextual toolbar. Different actions surface depending on what's selected — scenario, comparable, note, flag, GIS point. Fully specced for eng.
The product

Dark. Dense.
Deliberate.

The full product — GIS map, insights panel, AI assistant, action bar — all designed as a cohesive system. Enterprise tooling that analysts actually want to use.
