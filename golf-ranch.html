The problem

Golf Ranch is a fast-growing driving range franchise in Texas — anti-country-club, walk-in-friendly, and built for everyone from beginners to serious golfers. But their website wasn't doing the work. Traffic was coming in, conversions weren't following.

When I came on board, the site had no consistent lead funnel, no SEO strategy, and no way to measure what was actually driving visits. The ask: build a system that turns online interest into physical visits.

"Design the site like a product — not a brochure."

What I did

I started with a full audit: SEO, conversion flow, analytics setup. Then built out a complete redesign in Webflow — six location pages, a free bucket funnel, a thank-you page optimized for commitment, a careers page with intentional friction, and a blog system.

[ Before / After — Homepage redesign comparison ]

The free bucket funnel was the core lead-gen engine. Users fill out a form, land on a thank-you page with a location-specific Calendly embed — not as a real reservation, but as a psychological commitment device. When someone books a slot, they feel locked in. That feeling drives physical visit rates up.

[ Free Bucket Funnel — form → thank you page → Calendly ]

SEO overhaul

I rewrote every meta title and description across all six locations with city-specific keywords, set up Google Search Console and a sitemap, added LocalBusiness schema markup, and built a blog CMS for ongoing content. Within three months, organic traffic was up 25%.

[ GA4 / GSC organic traffic — before and after ]

Conversion optimization

Added a permanent sticky CTA button across the entire site driving traffic to the free bucket form. Built an exit intent pop-up. Ran A/B tests on headline copy. Analyzed GA4 funnels to identify drop-off points and iterated on each step.

[ Sticky CTA — mobile view + GA4 lift analysis ]

Results

Membership signups grew 4×. Organic traffic increased 25% across all six locations. The free bucket funnel became the primary lead-gen channel — and the system continues to improve with every A/B test.
