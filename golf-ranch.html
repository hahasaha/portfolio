Overview

A premium driving range deserves a premium site.

Golf Ranch operates six premium driving range locations across Texas, Missouri, Connecticut, and Arizona — featuring Toptracer shot-tracking technology, full bars, and a walk-in model that's growing fast.

When I came on, the site wasn't keeping up. 90% of traffic was mobile, but the experience was built for desktop. Lead capture was loose. Membership conversion was low. There was no SEO strategy.

My job was to fix all of it — design, build, and ship a site that could actually support growth.

Mobile-First Webflow Conversion Design Lead Generation SEO Design System

My scope: End-to-end design and Webflow build. I handled strategy, UX, visual design, copywriting, and implementation — working directly with the founders and their GTM lead.

The Site

Mobile-first, always.

With 90% of visitors coming in on phone, every design decision started on a 390px canvas. The desktop experience followed — not the other way around.

The Problem

The site was leaking every visitor it earned.

Walk-in traffic is Golf Ranch's model — no reservations, just show up. But the site wasn't giving people a reason to. No clear CTA above the fold. No lead capture to stay in touch. No local SEO to pull people in when they searched "driving range near me." The site looked like a brochure, not a growth tool.

Lead Generation

The free bucket — a 10,000/month lead machine.

The single highest-impact design decision: a persistent, always-visible sticky CTA button that drives visitors to the free bucket offer. Sign up, get a free bucket of balls on your first visit.

It sounds simple. The execution was everything — placement, copy, friction reduction, and the thank-you flow all had to work together. We rolled it out across all six location pages and the homepage.

The result: over 10,000 new leads per month, consistently.

Membership

4× more members — from one page redesign.

The Ranch Pass membership page was the second major conversion surface. The original version buried the value prop. Pricing was unclear. There was no urgency and no social proof.

I redesigned it from the ground up — leading with the savings math, surfacing testimonials, and making the sign-up flow frictionless on mobile.

Membership signups quadrupled.

Location Pages + SEO

Built to rank. Launched March 2026.

Each Golf Ranch location needed its own SEO-optimized page — structured for local search, consistent in design, and fast to publish as new locations open. I designed a scalable location page template in Webflow that the team can replicate in minutes.

The SEO push launched in March 2026. Organic traffic is already up 25% in the first weeks post-launch.

What's Next

Now building the Swing app.

The web results opened the door to a bigger product conversation. I'm now designing the Swing by Golf Ranch mobile app — a companion experience for members that brings session tracking, leaderboards, and location tools to their phone. 0→1, in progress.
