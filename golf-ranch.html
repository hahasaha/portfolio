The problem

Golf Ranch is a fast-growing driving range franchise in Texas — anti-country-club, walk-in-friendly, built for everyone. When I came on board, the site had no consistent lead funnel, no SEO strategy, and no way to measure what was actually driving visits. Traffic was coming in. Conversions weren't following.

"Design the site like a product — not a brochure."

What I built

I started with a full audit: SEO, conversion flow, analytics setup. Then rebuilt the site in Webflow — six location pages, a free bucket funnel, a thank-you page optimized for commitment, a careers page with intentional friction, and a blog CMS for ongoing content.

The free bucket funnel became the core lead-gen engine. Users fill out a form and land on a thank-you page with a location-specific Calendly embed — not a real reservation system, but a psychological commitment device. When someone books a time slot, they feel locked in. That feeling drives physical visit rates up.

SEO overhaul

I rewrote every meta title and description across all six locations with city-specific keywords, set up Google Search Console and a sitemap, added LocalBusiness schema markup, and built a blog CMS for ongoing content. Within three months, organic traffic was up 25%.

[ GA4 organic traffic — before and after ]

Conversion optimization

Added a permanent sticky CTA button across the entire site. Built an exit intent pop-up. Ran A/B tests on headline copy. Used GA4 funnel analysis to identify drop-off points and iterated on each step of the funnel.

Membership signups grew 4×. The free bucket funnel became the primary lead-gen channel and continues to improve with every test cycle.
