Enterprise tools built for experts under pressure.

Large creative teams were stuck with tools designed for individual contributors — not cross-functional workflows at scale. The challenge was designing interfaces that are intuitive for experts making high-stakes decisions, not just novices with time to learn.

The design challenge

Large enterprise teams deal with tools built for individual contributors that don't scale to cross-functional workflows. The design challenge: create interfaces that are intuitive for experts under decision-making pressure, not just novices with time to learn.

I focused on state legibility above everything else. In high-stakes workflows, users need to know exactly what the system is doing at any moment — what's active, what's changed, what needs their attention.

[ Violation details modal — comment, pin, and hide states ]

Key decisions

Every design decision was shaped by state legibility: badge placement, footer action ordering, how the AI assistant surfaces contextual help, and how the map-based interface communicates scale and scope. The action bar was constrained to scenario-level actions only — keeping the insights panel intact based on client feedback.
